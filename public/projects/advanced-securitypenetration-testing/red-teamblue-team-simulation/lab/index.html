Red Team & Blue Team Simulation#

Overview#

Red team vs. blue team cybersecurity exercises simulate real-world attacks and defensive responses. In this lab, you will conduct offensive attacks as a red team operator and detect/respond to threats as a blue team analyst.

What You Will Learn#

  • How attackers perform reconnaissance, exploitation, and post-exploitation
  • How defenders use SIEM tools to monitor logs, detect intrusions, and respond to attacks
  • How to simulate attack scenarios using MITRE ATT&CK techniques
  • How to write detection rules and analyze incident response logs

Lab Instructions#

1. Setting Up Your Environment#

You will need:

  • A Windows 10/11 VM (Attack Target)
  • A Kali Linux VM (Red Team System)
  • A Security Monitoring System (SIEM - Wazuh, Splunk, or ELK stack)

2. Red Team Phase (Attack Simulation)#

In this phase, you will simulate an attacker compromising a system.

Step 1: Perform Initial Reconnaissance#

Scan the network using Nmap to identify open ports.

nmap -sS -p 22,80,445 <TARGET_IP>

3. Blue Team Phase (Threat Detection & Response)#

In this phase, you will detect and respond to attacks using a SIEM tool.

Step 1: Detect Malicious Network Traffic#

Run Zeek or Wireshark to monitor for unusual SMB traffic.
